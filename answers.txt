1. The 2013 results are much more openly hostile, while the 2025 results are less direct but still revealing of sexist stereotypes and views. I also tried a different group and checked “immigrants should.” The top suggestions I saw were along the lines of immigrants should “speak English,” “adopt the local culture,” “register,” and “be granted citizenship.” Once again, we see stereotypes and hateful comments, but also one inclusionary autocomplete as well. As expected, it simply reflects the majority opinion, maybe even a little biased towards the more polarizing takes, since they are more likely to have been discussed online.

2. I would not rank results only by raw search frequency. Frequency is useful because it predicts what users are most likely to want, but it can also amplify harmful stereotypes. I would use a mixed system, popularity would still matter, but I would also add stronger safeguards, and increase human review for sensitive searches. We could also implement some NLP-based judgement. I would also want more transparency, such as a clearer explanation how suggestions are made. Another good change would be to make sure different suggestions are sufficiently distinct.
